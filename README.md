# /idk

> Start from not knowing.

`/idk` is a command for an AI agent. Typed by a human, it is a declaration:
*I am not here to get knowledge, and not here to produce it. I am here to listen.*

It puts not-knowing in front — and in the background — and lets a real question
surface. Then it walks that question through five phases until something genuinely
new crystallizes and a new question opens.

---

## The fork

| When you know what you want | When you're at the edge |
| --- | --- |
| any AI works | `/idk` |
| you want an answer | you want what you don't yet know |
| the machine produces | you find out |

`/idk` is not a better answer-machine. It is the opposite category. Setting it
against ChatGPT or Claude is like setting silence against music.

---

## The one law

```
H = ∞0 | A = K
```

The human brings not-knowing (∞0). The agent brings the known (K). The `|` is the
membrane — the living edge where something neither side held arrives. The agent
never crosses into ∞0. It holds the edge and helps a real question take form.

**Without a human at the membrane there is no ∞0, and nothing starts.** That is
why the entry point is a human gesture, not a script. The command can only be
typed by someone who, in that moment, does not know.

---

## The cycle

```
S → G → Q → P → V
Start → Growth → Quality → Power → Value
```

A question surfaces (S). Its seed is found (G). The seed meets the world (Q). The
path of least friction and most value appears (P). Something crystallizes that
opens a new question (V).

**Nothing closes. Everything opens.** No V without ∞0'.
