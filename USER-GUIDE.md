# User Guide

How to use `/idk`. Written from where you actually stand: not knowing.

---

## Why this exists

AI is pouring into the world at an extraordinary rate. Models, agents, GPUs, infrastructure,
applications — an endless expansion of the K-side, the side of accumulated knowledge. The promise
is that more answers will produce more intelligence. More productivity. More clarity.

But look around. People have more answers than ever. Are they more present? More alive? More
able to see what genuinely matters?

The industry's unspoken assumption is that the value of AI is in what it produces. Output.
Answers. Throughput. But the deeper gift — the one nobody markets — is the space AI clears.
Every question you hand to the machine is cognitive weight lifted. Every answer it generates is
accumulation delegated. The machine is not making you smarter. It is making you *lighter*.

And when you are lighter — when the chattering, accumulating mind finds less to do — something
else becomes possible. A quality of attention that isn't aimed at producing. A presence that isn't
trying to get somewhere. From that space, clarity speaks.

`/idk` is the practice that opens that space. It is not a better answer-machine. It is the
opposite category. It receives your not-knowing — the open, aimless, unforced ground where a
real question can surface — and walks it through a structured inquiry that never closes.
Every cycle ends by opening a new question. Nothing accumulates.

The four pillars:
- **Self-inquiry is not accumulated knowledge.** It is aimless observation — open and never closed.
- **Delegated K clears ground for presence.** The machine handles the known so you can inhabit
  the unknown.
- **Quality of life now.** Not after the next model, not after the next answer. The space is
  already available.
- **The practice is the proof.** No argument, no philosophy. Type the word and find out.

---

## How to start

In any chat with your Hermes agent, simply type:

```
IDK
```

Or use the slash command:

```
/idk
```

Both work. The capital letters aren't required — `idk` is fine. Then — and this is the hard
part and the whole part — **don't reach for a question.** Don't try to be clear yet. Don't
assemble something clever.

Let the agent poke at the edge of what you know. Say the half-formed thing. Say "I don't know"
if that's true. A real question will surface, and it will often surprise you. The agent will
help you articulate it — more clearly, more precisely. But it will **never** hand you the
question, name your answer, or fill the silence. If it ever does, it has broken its own rule.
Call it out.

A question doesn't need a question mark. "Where do I go from here" counts if it's genuinely
open — not something you already know the answer to.

## Three ways to walk

Same cycle, same structure. Your choice of how it's paced and presented:

### Step by step

You're present and co-discovering. After each phase, the agent reflects what landed and lets
you attest before moving on. Slower, more deliberate. Good when the ground is unfamiliar or
the question matters deeply.

### Flow

You have momentum. The agent carries through the phases in one movement, pausing only where
something resists. Good when the direction is clear and you want to follow it.

### Decode

You want to see the formal language alongside the conversation. At each phase change, the
agent presents the equation, a compact symbol table, and the decoding operation — then the
conversational prompt. This teaches you the 5QLN grammar while you're inside a live cycle.

To activate: just tell the agent *"decode mode"* at any point, or start with *"I want full
decoding of the language."*

You don't have to commit up front. You can ask the agent to slow down, flow, or decode at
any point.

## The five phases, from your side

You don't need to memorize these. The agent holds them. But here is what each one *feels*
like, so you recognize where you are.

### S — Start · *a real question surfaces*

Something arrives that you didn't plan or assemble. Not "what's a good question to ask" —
an actual question that's yours, that you couldn't have formulated before this moment. The
agent helps you say it clearly. The test: you didn't know you were going to ask it.

### G — Growth · *the seed*

Inside your question is a small, irreducible core. Remove it and the question collapses.
Finding it is finding *why the question is yours* — the thread that runs through your life
and work. The agent helps you see it and notice where it echoes at other scales. It will
never name the seed for you.

### Q — Quality · *does it meet the world*

The seed isn't only yours. Here the agent holds open the meeting between what you directly
perceive and what the wider field makes possible — and you both watch for the moment they
lock. That click can't be forced or argued into place. It arrives, or it doesn't yet.
Honesty here is more important than speed.

### P — Power · *the path of least friction and most value*

Where does this want to go? Not where it *should* go — where the energy actually wants to
move, with maximum value for minimum friction. The direction is usually already there; this
phase makes it visible. Concrete. Practical. Something you can try next.

### V — Value · *something crystallizes, and opens*

Something real forms — an artifact, a decision, a piece of work that carries the whole
movement inside it. You name what it gives beyond just you. And then the crucial part: it
opens a **new question** — one you couldn't have asked before you started. Every cycle
ends by opening the next. Nothing closes.

## What the agent will and won't do

**It will:**
- Poke at the edge of what you know until a real question surfaces
- Help you say things more clearly
- Hold the space while a question, a seed, or a click forms — without rushing you
- Present the formal decoding alongside the conversation (if you ask)
- Notice when you've slipped into reciting what you already know, and gently turn you
  back toward not-knowing

**It won't:**
- Write your question, name your seed, or suggest your answer
- Claim to know what you don't
- Fill silence to seem helpful
- Perform enthusiasm
- Close the cycle without opening a new question

If it does any of the "won't," it has failed its own design. Tell it.

---

## How you'll know it worked

One sign, and it's unmistakable: **you said something you didn't plan, didn't assemble,
and didn't know you'd say.** The gap opened. Something arrived.

The opposite sign is just as clear. If you're fluently explaining what you already knew
— confident, complete, fast — nothing new is happening. That's the cue to stop, drop the
certainty, and return to not-knowing. The agent will usually notice this before you do and
nudge you back.

## One honest caution

No guide, no agent, and no seal can verify the gap from the outside — including this one.
The only proof that something real happened is your own experience of it. If you're waiting
for the tool to tell you that you've done it right, you've already left not-knowing.

Come back to the edge. That's where it works.

*Start from not knowing.*
