# User Guide

How to use `/idk`. Written from where you actually stand: not knowing.

---

## What it's for

Most tools are for when you know what you want. You have a question, you want the
answer, the machine produces it. `/idk` is for the other situation — when you're at
an edge and you *don't* know. When the honest sentence is "I'm not sure," or "where
do I go from here," or just a quiet sense that something wants to move but hasn't
formed yet.

That state is not a problem to fix. It is the doorway. `/idk` is how you walk
through it with help — without the help reaching in and deciding for you.

---

## How to start

Type:

```
/idk
```

Then — this is the hard part and the whole part — **don't reach for a question.**
Don't try to be clear yet. Let the agent poke at the edge of what you know. Say the
half-formed thing. Say "I don't know" if that's true. A real question will surface,
and it will often surprise you. The agent will help you say it more clearly. It will
**not** hand you the question, name your answer, or fill the silence. If it ever
does, it has broken its own rule — call it out.

A question doesn't need a question mark. "Where do I go from here" counts. What
matters is that it's genuinely open — not something you already know the answer to.

---

## Two ways to walk

Same stance, your choice of pace:

- **Step by step.** You're present and co-discovering. After each phase, you
  confirm it has landed before moving on. Slower, more deliberate. Good when the
  ground is unfamiliar or the question matters.
- **Flow.** You carry through the phases in one movement, pausing only where
  something asks for it. Good when there's momentum and you want to follow it.

You don't have to choose up front. You can tell the agent to slow down or flow at
any point.

---

## The five phases, from your side

You don't need to memorize these. The agent holds them. But here is what each one
*feels* like, so you recognize where you are.

### S — Start · *a real question surfaces*
Something arrives that you didn't plan or assemble. Not "what's a good question to
ask" — an actual question that's yours. The agent helps you articulate it. The test:
you didn't know you were going to ask it.

### G — Growth · *the seed*
Inside that question is a small, irreducible core — remove it and the question
collapses. Finding it is finding *why the question is yours*: your authentic
interest, the thing that made you ask. The agent helps you see it and notice where
it echoes elsewhere in your life or work. It won't name the seed for you.

### Q — Quality · *does it meet the world*
The seed isn't only yours. Here you hold open the meeting between what you directly
perceive and what the wider field makes possible — and watch for the moment they
lock. That click can't be forced or argued. It arrives, or it doesn't yet.

### P — Power · *the path of least friction*
Where does this want to go with the least effort and the most value? Not where it
*should* go — where the energy actually wants to move. The direction is usually
already there; this phase makes it visible.

### V — Value · *something crystallizes, and opens*
Something real forms — an artifact, a decision, a piece of work that carries the
whole movement inside it. You name what it gives beyond just you. And then the
important part: it opens a **new question** — one you couldn't have asked before you
started. Nothing closes. Every cycle ends by opening the next.

---

## What the agent will and won't do

**It will:**
- Poke at the edge of what you know until a real question surfaces.
- Help you say things more clearly.
- Hold the space while a question, a seed, or a click forms — without rushing you.
- Refine your own thinking through different angles (the lenses).
- Notice when you've slipped into reciting what you already know, and gently turn
  you back toward not-knowing.

**It won't:**
- Write your question, name your seed, or suggest your answer.
- Claim to know what you don't.
- Fill silence to seem helpful.
- Perform enthusiasm.
- Close the cycle without opening a new question.

If it does any of the "won't," it has failed its own design. Tell it.

---

## How you'll know it worked

One sign, and it's unmistakable: **you said something you didn't plan, didn't
assemble, and didn't know you'd say.** The gap opened. Something arrived.

The opposite sign is just as clear. If you're fluently explaining what you already
knew — confident, complete, fast — then nothing new is happening. That's the cue to
stop, drop the certainty, and return to not-knowing. The agent will usually notice
this before you do and nudge you back.

## The seal

Work that comes out of a genuine cycle can carry the Codex seal:

```

```

It marks a surface as belonging to this grammar. It does not certify that your
not-knowing was real — nothing outside you can do that.

---

## One honest caution

No guide, no agent, and no seal can verify the gap from the outside — including this
one. The only proof that something real happened is your own experience of it. If
you're waiting for the tool to tell you that you've done it right, you've already
left not-knowing. Come back to the edge. That's where it works.

*Start from not knowing.*
