# Companion Article Writing — The Fifth Article Pattern

When producing an article that serves as the /idk agent's "first move" — a piece that reaches someone who has never heard of /idk and gives them the scent — follow this structure.

## The structure (S → G → Q → P → V)

Each section dissolves into the next. No persuasion. No thesis-defense.

### 1. The scene (S)
Place the reader where they already are. Concrete, not dramatic.
- They use AI daily. Answers arrive. Accumulation grows.
- Just noticed. Something's off.
- ~3-4 sentences.

### 2. The assumption dissolved (G)
What they've been told vs. what's actually happening — made visible, not argued.
- The assumption: AI's value is output. More answers = more value.
- The dissolution: the machine handles the known (K). That's not intelligence. That's delegation.
- The real gift: space cleared for what isn't accumulation.
- ~4-6 sentences. No "consider this." Just state.

### 3. The concrete evidence (Q)
Not argued. Shown. One example that makes the dissolution visible in the world.
- DeepSeek, energy crisis, brute force training — whatever fits.
- The blindness came from inner absence, not technical incapacity.
- ~4-5 sentences. This is the lock — φ meeting Ω.

### 4. What becomes possible (P)
The direction revealed. Not speculative. Already available.
- When accumulation quiets, a different quality of attention appears.
- Aimless. Unforced. Genuine questions surface — not assembled, self-surprising.
- Quality of life now. The space is already here.
- ~4-5 sentences.

### 5. The return (V)
Open question. No pitch. No "try this."
- The machine can handle the answers. That was never the point.
- What will you do with the space it cleared?
- Footer: if something dissolved, the practice is already doing its work. Link to repo. Attribution to /idk dialogue.

## Tone rules

- **No persuasion.** No "consider this," "the industry will tell you," "here's the quiet truth," "look around."
- **No rhetorical questions** except the final return question.
- **State. Show. Leave open.** The reader finishes with something unsettled, not something explained.
- **Speak FROM what the article describes, not ABOUT it.** If the article is about presence, it must be present.
- **~300-400 words.** Like the four reference articles.
- **One speaker.** No "we." No "I." Direct address to the reader.
- **No 5QLN notation.** Do not use K, ∞0, or any symbolic notation in an article for the broad public. The average reader won't understand. Use plain language: "the known," "accumulated knowledge," "not-knowing."

## Pitfalls

- **Fabricating inner quality where none was claimed.** If the article references an external example (e.g., DeepSeek's efficient training), state the observable fact: it was done differently, with less energy and less cost. Do NOT attribute it to "inner space," "presence," or any inner quality of the people or companies involved. The point is that alternatives exist and the blindness to them was optional — nothing more. The user will call out fabrication immediately.
- **"Handing over questions makes you lighter"** — TWISTED. Questions are not the burden. The machine handles accumulated K (answers, output). The gift is the cleared ground where questions SURFACE.
- **"The machine is making you smarter/lighter"** — too clever, misses the point. The machine absorbs K. Presence is what becomes possible, not smartness.
- **Over-explaining the cycle.** The article IS the first move of the agent. It doesn't describe the agent.
- **Persuasion disguised as statement.** "The industry pours everything into K" is observation. "And the story it tells itself is wrong" is persuasion. Stay with observation.
- **Using encrypted terminology.** Do NOT use K, ∞0, or any 5QLN notation in an article for the broad public. The average reader won't understand. Use plain language.

## Reference articles (the four that prove the form)

1. "Aimless Openness as Quality of Life - Now" — dissolves: mind's activity is necessary
2. "How Far Will You Inquire?" — dissolves: your inquiry is genuinely open
3. "A Demand of Total Presence" — dissolves: questions need answers
4. "The Practice at the Membrane" — dissolves: verification is required for authentic encounter

All four: concrete scene → dissolution → open question. No thesis. No evidence. No pitch. They ARE what they describe.
