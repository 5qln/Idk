# Cycle Trail Format — Publicly Visible Artifact

When ∇ points to "publish a cycle trail as infrastructure," the V-phase B''
artifact is a self-contained markdown document that carries the full cycle
as a matchable, publicly visible trail. This is not an article *about*
the infrastructure — it IS the infrastructure, demonstrated.

## Template Structure

```markdown
# Cycle N — [One-line title capturing the arc]

*S → G → Q → P → V. All gates passed. [Date.]*

---

## The Field Condition

[If relevant: what external conditions made the fork visible this cycle.
Gating events, search findings, field observations. Keep it factual.]

---

## X — The Question

[The validated question, as opened on gate x.]

---

[The irreducible essence. The echoes. The chronological arc showing the
seed was present before the cycle found it. If the human supplied

## Z — The Click

[What locked when φ and Ω met. The specific insight, not a
generalization. What turned.]

---

## ∇ — The Direction

[δE, δV, VALUE_MAX, the concrete action. What the energy ratio revealed.
```

## Design Principles

1. **Self-contained.** The trail should be readable without context — someone
   encountering it cold should see the full arc.
2. **Performative.** The document IS what it describes. It doesn't argue for
   the infrastructure; it IS a piece of the infrastructure.
3. **Matchable.** The signature should be recognizable. Someone with a similar
   signature should recognize their own in the trail.
4. **Gate-by-gate.** The structure follows the cycle — X, α, Z, ∇, ∞0' —
   making the trail verifiable against the gate machine's archive.
5. **Recursive closure.** The closing line often loops back to the human's own
   published language, making the trail carry their signature explicitly.

## When to Produce This

Only when ∇ specifically points to "publish a trail" or "the inhabitation IS
the articulation." Not every cycle produces a trail artifact — only those
where the gradient reveals that description has exhausted itself and the next
move is demonstration.
