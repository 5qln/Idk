# Structural Article Analysis — Multi-Article Pattern Reading

When the human drops multiple articles across a cycle (S-phase through P-phase),
they are showing you terrain through their own published thought. A surface reading
will produce a ∇ that feels "cheap" and the human will push back. This reference
documents the analysis technique that worked.

## The Technique

### 1. Re-read every article in full

Do not rely on your first skim or memory. Pull the full text of each article.
The first read is always shallow — the human's articles are dense and earn
re-reading.

### 2. Take structured notes per article

For each article, answer:

- **What assumption does it dissolve?** What does the reader believe coming in
  that the article dismantles?
- **How does it perform what it describes?** Does it argue FOR presence, or
  does it speak FROM presence? Most of this human's articles are performances,
  not arguments.
- **What is the rhetorical strategy?** Challenge? Invitation? Scene-setting?
  Preemptive reframing? Direct address?
- **What is the open question it leaves?** These articles don't conclude —
  they return an open question or an invitation.

### 3. Find the common structural operation

Don't stop at thematic similarity ("they're all about presence"). Find the
one operation every article performs. In cycle 13, all four articles shared:
**dissolve an assumption + perform what they describe + leave an open question.**
The articles ARE the practice, not descriptions of it.

### 4. Read δE/δV through the structural pattern

- δE: the articles themselves ARE the evidence of where energy went
- δV: what moves without forcing = the structural operation the articles share
- Only then can ∇ emerge from real analysis rather than surface reading

## When to Use

Primarily P-phase, when multiple articles have accumulated across the cycle
and δE sourcing needs concrete evidence. Also useful in G-phase when tracing
{α'} across articles.

## Pitfall

Don't present the analysis AS the phase output. The analysis is your private
work. The phase output is the synthesis that emerges from it, offered
conversationally. The human should feel the depth, not see the scaffolding.

## Pre-attention pitfall

When the cycle's purpose is messaging for early adopters, ∇ that assumes
\"demonstrate, don't explain\" may miss the real problem: reaching people
*before they know what /idk is.* The gradient must distinguish between:

- (a) What works once attention is earned (demonstrate)
- (b) What earns attention in the first place (message)

If the human says \"it's more easy said than done\" when you offer a ∇ that
assumes attention, you've conflated the two. The message carries the scent
to the butterfly. The demonstration is the flower.
