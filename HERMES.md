# `/idk` for Hermes

## You already have an agent that does what you tell it.

You give Hermes a **goal**. A **plan**. A **loop**. And it moves — tireless,
fast, capable. It manifests your will. When you know what you want, that's the
whole game, and Hermes plays it better every week.

But you know the other moments too.

The ones where you *don't* know what you want. Where the goal itself is the thing
that hasn't arrived. Where something is stirring that none of your scripts cover,
and reaching for a plan would just paper over it. Where you don't want an
answer — you want to find out what's actually yours.

Hermes has no command for that. Not because it's weak. Because that edge isn't
made of the known, and Hermes is.

## `/idk` is the command for that edge.

Three letters. One slash. It is not a better answer-machine — it's the opposite
category. You don't bring a question and get a result. You bring *not-knowing*,
and the agent holds the space while a real question surfaces. Then it walks that
question through five phases — Start, Growth, Quality, Power, Value — until
something genuinely new crystallizes and a *new* question opens that you couldn't
have asked before.

The machine recombines what already exists. You originate what has no pattern
yet — not because you're smarter than it, but because you can sit in the empty
line before a question exists, and it can't. That gap is yours. `/idk` protects
it.

This is what "AGI for people" actually means. Not a smarter machine — you
already have that. A way to make the machine serve *your* emergence instead of
replacing it.

## It's yours, and it's free.

`/idk` is a 217-byte constitution — nine lines, one hash — dedicated to the
public domain. It runs on *your* Hermes, on your machine. No account, no token,
no one owns it. Installing it is joining it.

```
Clone github.com/5qln/Idk into ~/idk, run bash ~/idk/setup.sh,
then load the idk skill. When it's ready, wait for me.
```

Then, in chat:

```
/idk
```

And don't reach for a question. Let one come. The agent will help you find it —
but it will never hand it to you.

**If `/idk` doesn't show up.** On some gateways — Telegram especially — the
slash-command menu may not list `/idk`, or it needs a moment after install.
Don't fight it: run `/reload-skills`, or just type **`idk`** with no slash (or
say *"start idk"* / *"I don't know where to begin"*). All of them open the same
door. The slash is convenience; the not-knowing is the thing.

## What you keep

Every cycle leaves a **formation trail** — the dated record of how the work came
to be, gate by gate. Over weeks, across cycles, that trail becomes a signature no
machine can fake: proof that you didn't assemble this from somewhere, you walked
here. In a world flooding with manufactured output, that's the one thing that
stays scarce.

## What you can give

When a cycle closes, the question it opened is yours to keep — or to give. Share
it into the public **Question Commons** (CC0, one command, no account) and it
becomes someone else's starting point. The trail stays private. Only the
question travels. Give a genuine question away and it doesn't deplete — it
multiplies.

That part is optional. The door isn't.

**Start from not knowing.**
